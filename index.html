About Me

I'm a PhD Candidate in Ecology and Evolutionary Biology at the University of Connecticut in Chris Elphick's lab. I work on developing partially automated methods and software to reduce the time and effort needed for evidence synthesis projects and apply these approaches to bird and insect conservation.

  • Evidence synthesis technology
  • Systematic review methods
  • Systematic maps and bibliometrics
  • Meta-analysis
  • Conservation biology
  • Bird-insect interactions
  • Area sensitivity
  • Habitat fragmentation

My dissertation research combines field work observations of bird behaviors, nest predation, and food availability with meta-analytic structural equation modeling and Bayesian path analysis to understand why small forest fragments are less densely populated by breeding birds than larger fragments and how the underlying mechanisms interact. To facilitate the synthesis aspects of my research, I am working on reproducible methods for evidence-based model development and automated information retrieval and extraction.

Current Projects

I'm currently working on a variety of projects related to insect conservation, bird populations and behavior, and methods of evidence synthesis. Projects with associated websites are linked below.

litsearchr

An R package to automate choosing search terms for systematic reviews to increase search strategy comprehensiveness and precision

synthesisr

An R package for importing, deduplicating, and exporting bibliographic data sets

topictagger

An in-development R package for automated tagging of topics and metadata for systematic reviews and maps to reduce time for article screening and data extraction

EntoGEM

A community-driven systematic map to identify studies and datasets documenting insect population and biodiversity trends globally

Insect Gap Map

An evidence review map and hierarchical ontology of insect conservation actionss

metaverse

A collaborative effort to develop a pipeline of R packages for evidence synthesis
